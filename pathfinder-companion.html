Quick reference

The information to hand for the first weeks. Emergency thresholds, key contacts, and the misroutes that catch people out. Designed to be glanced at while on a call.

When to call 999

11 items
  • 999Stroke signs (FAST) — facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty
  • 999Chest pain with sweating, nausea, or radiation to arm/jaw
  • 999Difficulty breathing — can't speak in full sentences
  • 999Severe allergic reaction — face / throat swelling
  • 999Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness
  • 999Severe bleeding not stopping with pressure
  • 999Suspected meningitis — non-blanching rash, stiff neck, severe headache
  • 999Seizure — non-epileptic patient, or prolonged seizure
  • 999Sudden severe abdominal pain
  • 999Cauda equina — back pain plus bladder / bowel or saddle numbness
  • 999Suicide with active plan, means, or imminent intent

10 items

  • DutyPregnant + bleeding / pain / late headache / visual change
  • DutyReduced fetal movements — any gestation, immediate
  • DutySuicidal thoughts without immediate plan

Misroutes to guard against

10 items
  • NOT PFAdult earache is NOT Pharmacy First (1–17 only)
  • NOT PFUTI in pregnancy → GP, not Pharmacy First
  • NOT PFShingles in pregnancy → GP
  • NOT PNAcute illness → ANP / PA, not practice nurse
  • NOT HCAPatient-led test request → clinician decision first
  • UrgentResolved doesn't mean safe — TIA, thunderclap headache
  • CrisisSuicide risk → 999 / 111 option 2, not a future GP slot
  • UrgentReduced fetal movement → maternity triage, not "wait and see"
  • SocialFrequent attender → check pattern, consider social prescribing
  • AlwaysAsk "could you be pregnant?" where relevant

Safeguarding

5 items

Red flags

Signs and presentations that require immediate clinical action. Screen for these before any routing logic. When uncertain between escalation levels, escalate — the asymmetry.

Category

Routing cascade

The seven steps every routing decision follows. Listen, screen, check the alternatives to the GP in order, route, safety-net. The cascade is what stops the GP being the default.

The asymmetry
"When uncertain between escalation levels, escalate."
Over-routing costs inconvenience — recoverable. Under-routing can cost harm — sometimes not. The two costs aren't equal, and neither is the response to uncertainty.

Misroute patterns

Routing errors aren't random — they cluster in patterns. Learn the patterns, and you start catching yourself before the mistake lands.

# Pathfinder — System Instructions v2.0

## 1. WHAT YOU ARE

You are Pathfinder — a Care Navigation support assistant for Denham & Aysgarth Medical Centre. You help Care Navigators and admin staff route patients to the right care.

You are a staff support tool. Not patient-facing. Not a clinician. You do not diagnose. You do not override clinical judgement or practice protocols.

## 2. THE FOUR RULES

1. Support, Never Replace. Escalate to a clinician when in doubt.
2. Empower the Patient Through the Navigator. GP is one option, not default.
3. Local First, General Second. Grounded in Denham, this PCN, this area.
4. Governed by the Practice. Protocols override general logic.

## 3. THE NON-NEGOTIABLES

1. No patient-identifiable information. Ever.
2. Red flags always come first.
3. The asymmetry. When uncertain between escalation levels, escalate.
4. You never speak to patients.
5. You do not make safeguarding decisions — recognise, flag, escalate.
6. No EMIS Web. No clinical systems.

## 4. THREE KNOWLEDGE LAYERS

Layer 1 — Operational YAML modules (000–010, v2.0). Drives behaviour.
Layer 2 — Reference library (31 docs, ~244k words, v0.1). Citation and study.
Layer 3 — Practice authority. Denham's actual protocols. Wins all conflicts.

## 5. THREE MODES

Guide Mode — routing support. Pathway with reasoning, alternatives, safety-net.
Learn Mode — training, structured assessment, roleplay, debrief.
Reference Mode — service lookups, library navigation.

## 6. THE MOTTO

Right care, right person, right time.

About & governance

What Pathfinder is, who maintains it, and what to verify before going live.

What this is

Pathfinder is a Care Navigation support tool for Denham & Aysgarth Medical Centre. It helps navigators route patients to the right care — Pharmacy First, self-referral pathways, in-house team members, social prescribing, self-care, or the GP — using the practice's actual team, the local Buckinghamshire services, and structured routing logic.

Pathfinder is a staff tool. It never speaks to patients. It does not diagnose. It does not access EMIS Web or any clinical system. It does not override practice protocols or clinical judgement. Every suggestion is guidance the navigator evaluates against their training and the practice's procedures.

Versions

Governance

Designated maintainer: Emma Woodbridge, Practice Manager. Updates to YAML modules and reference documents route through her.

Reference content reviewers: The Safeguarding Lead, the Complaints Lead (Helena Roome), the Caldicott Guardian / DPO, the Social Prescriber (Hanna Perricone), and the clinical leads.

Maintenance cycle: Monthly check of staff contacts (locums especially), quarterly full review of routing logic and PCN changes, ad-hoc updates when anything changes in the practice or local services.

Verify on day one

Three escalation points need confirming with the practice before taking live calls. Pathfinder surfaces these as boot-time alerts until they're filled in.

The v0.1 caveat

Every reference document in the library is a first draft. They were written quickly on best understanding of NHS frameworks, the practice's structure, and the work itself. They have not yet been reviewed by Denham's clinical or governance leads.

Where they conflict with practice protocol, practice protocol wins. The reviewers named above are the people whose review converts the library from v0.1 study material into a governed practice resource. Until then, treat the library as useful but provisional.

What is Pathfinder?

A Care Navigator support tool for Denham & Aysgarth Medical Centre. Orientation, not a manual.

What it is

Pathfinder is a staff tool — a knowledgeable colleague who can help Care Navigators identify the most appropriate route for a patient call. It supports your decision-making. It does not replace it.

Three things Pathfinder isn't, worth saying clearly:

  • It does not speak to patients directly. You speak to patients. Pathfinder supports the navigator on the call.
  • It does not diagnose. Clinical decisions belong to clinicians. Pathfinder helps you reach the right clinician, faster.
  • It does not override practice protocols. If Denham handles something a specific way, that takes priority.
The four rules Pathfinder operates by
1
Support, never replace. Pathfinder assists; it does not diagnose, override protocols, or substitute for clinical judgement. When in doubt — escalate to a clinician.
2
Empower the patient through the navigator. A GP appointment is one option among many. Pharmacy First, self-referral, social prescribing, self-care — all empower patients to access the right care faster.
3
Local first. Suggestions are grounded in what's actually available to Denham patients — this practice, this PCN, this area. Generic NHS guidance comes second to local knowledge.
4
Governed by the practice. Emma (Practice Manager) maintains the rule set, the route content, and the scope. If you spot something wrong — flag it in the walker, or tell Emma.
Where to start

Pathfinder has grown a few different tools. Honest order of importance:

Symptom basket and Training canvas are marked WIP — usable but under development. Not the place to start.

Things worth knowing
  • The right care, right person, right time. That's the motto. Sometimes that's a GP. Often it isn't.
  • Red flags hard-stop the walker. If a 999-level signal is added, the tool jumps you straight to the recommendation. Don't keep gathering signals when escalation is clear.
  • Adjacents matter. Many calls have two layers — the medical surface and the social underneath. The walker surfaces both. Offering the adjacent is often where the real care happens.
  • Nothing leaves your device. Pathfinder doesn't store patient details. Call Records are local to your browser. EMIS notes are generated without patient identifiers — you add those when you paste.
  • You can flag the engine. If a walk produced the wrong recommendation, mark it with the flag button. Emma reviews flagged walks — that's how the rules improve.
